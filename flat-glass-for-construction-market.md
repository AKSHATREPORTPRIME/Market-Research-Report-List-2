<p><p>The flat glass market for construction primarily consists of two types: rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is manufactured by passing molten glass between two large metal rollers, resulting in a uniform thickness. This type of glass is commonly used for applications requiring fire resistance and safety. On the other hand, float glass is produced by pouring molten glass onto a bath of molten tin, creating a smooth and distortion-free surface. It is widely used in architectural and commercial construction due to its excellent optical clarity and energy efficiency.</p></p>
