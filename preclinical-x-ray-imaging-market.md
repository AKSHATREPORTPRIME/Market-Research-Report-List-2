<p><p>Preclinical X-ray Imaging refers to the use of X-ray technology in preclinical research on animals. There are two types of markets within this field: Structural Imaging and Functional Imaging. Structural Imaging involves capturing detailed images of anatomical structures to identify abnormalities or measure physiological growth. Functional Imaging, on the other hand, focuses on capturing dynamic processes within the body, such as blood flow or metabolism, to study organ function and disease progression. Both types of imaging play key roles in preclinical research to aid in understanding disease mechanisms and developing new treatments.</p></p>
