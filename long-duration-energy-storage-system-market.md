<p><p>The long duration energy storage system market is comprised of various types of storage technologies. Pumped storage involves storing energy by pumping water to a higher elevation, then releasing it to generate electricity when needed. LAES (liquid air energy storage) relies on cooling air into liquid form and later expanding it to drive a turbine. CAES (compressed air energy storage) stores energy in compressed air and releases it to generate power. Molten salt energy storage, flow batteries, and Li-Ion batteries store energy chemically. Power-to-gas technology converts electricity into hydrogen or methane for storage. Other technologies include flywheels, thermal energy storage, and cryogenic energy storage.</p></p>
