<p><p>A liferaft is a type of inflatable raft designed to provide emergency evacuation for people in situations where water poses a threat to their safety, such as in the event of a shipwreck or an aircraft ditching. Liferafts are typically made of durable materials capable of withstanding harsh marine environments, and they are equipped with necessary survival items such as food, water, and signaling devices. They are an integral part of marine safety equipment and are often required to be carried on board ships, offshore platforms, and aircraft.</p><p>The future outlook for the liferaft market looks promising, with a projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.7% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the stringent safety regulations imposed by various maritime organizations and government bodies worldwide are driving the demand for liferafts. Ships and other marine vessels are required to comply with these regulations, thereby increasing the adoption of liferafts.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing number of offshore projects in the oil and gas industry is expected to contribute to the market growth. Offshore platforms and rigs require liferafts as part of their safety measures, and the expansion of offshore exploration and production activities is creating a demand for liferafts.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising focus on passenger safety in the maritime and aviation industries is also expected to drive the liferaft market. Increasing travel and tourism activities, as well as the growth of commercial airlines, contribute to the demand for liferafts in these sectors.</p><p>Overall, the liferaft market is set to witness steady growth in the coming years due to regulatory requirements, offshore activities, and the need for passenger safety. The market players will likely focus on product innovation, improved durability, and enhanced safety features to cater to the evolving demands of various industries.</p></p>
