<p><p>Milling robots are categorized based on their number of axes, which determines their range of motion and versatility. 4-axis robots can move along three linear axes and rotate around one rotational axis, allowing for simple milling operations. 5-axis robots have an additional rotational axis, enabling them to perform more complex milling tasks from various angles. 6-axis robots offer even greater flexibility by incorporating an additional linear axis, allowing for more precise and intricate milling operations. 7-axis robots possess an additional rotational wrist joint, expanding their reach and capabilities further. Other market segments may include robots with unique combinations of axes or specialized features tailored to specific milling applications.</p></p>
