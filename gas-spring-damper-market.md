<p><p>A gas spring is a mechanical device that utilizes compressed gas to store potential energy, which is then used to provide a controlled force or motion. It consists of a cylinder filled with gas and a piston that moves within the cylinder. Gas springs are widely used in various applications such as automotive, furniture, industrial, aerospace, and medical equipment, providing benefits like smooth and controlled motion, adjustable force, and space optimization.</p><p>On the other hand, a damper is a device that is used to absorb and dissipate energy, reducing the oscillations or vibrations in a system. Dampers can be either hydraulic or pneumatic and are commonly used in automotive suspension systems, doors, and industrial machinery to control the movement and prevent sudden jolts or impacts.</p><p>The gas spring and damper market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing demand from various industries.
