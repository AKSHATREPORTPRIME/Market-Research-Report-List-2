<p><p>Botrytis tests are used to detect the presence of Botrytis cinerea, a fungus that causes plant diseases. Lateral flow analysis is a commonly used type of Botrytis test, where a sample is applied to a test strip and the results are visible through the appearance or absence of colored lines. Fluorescence enzyme immunoassay involves using specific antibodies to detect the fungus, producing fluorescence in the presence of Botrytis. Other types of Botrytis tests may involve different techniques or technologies to detect the fungus, but the overall goal is to identify and control its presence in plants.</p></p>
