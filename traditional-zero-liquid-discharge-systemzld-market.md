<p><p>Traditional Zero Liquid Discharge System (ZLD) is a water treatment system that aims to eliminate liquid discharge from an industrial process by recovering all wastewater and converting it into solid waste. The ZLD system consists of various market types, including Preprocessing, Filter, Evaporation, and Crystallization. </p><p>Preprocessing involves the removal of large particles and contaminants from the wastewater before further treatment. The Filter stage utilizes various filtration techniques to remove smaller particles and impurities. Evaporation is the process of heating the wastewater to evaporate the liquid content, leaving behind concentrated solids. Crystallization involves further drying the concentrated solids to form crystal-like substances that can be safely disposed of. These market types collectively enable industries to achieve zero liquid discharge effectively.</p></p>
