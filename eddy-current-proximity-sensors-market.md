<p><p>Eddy current proximity sensors are non-contact devices that are used to detect the presence and position of metallic objects without physical contact. These sensors operate on the principle of electromagnetic induction. They generate an alternating magnetic field that induces eddy currents in the conductive material of the target object. <p><p>Eddy current proximity sensors are widely used in various industries for non-contact distance and position sensing applications. The market for these sensors can be classified into two types: split type eddy current sensors and integrated eddy current sensors. </p><p>Split type eddy current sensors consist of a probe and a separate electronics unit. The probe is placed close to the target object, and the electronics unit analyzes the eddy currents induced in the target to determine its position or distance.</p><p>Integrated eddy current sensors, on the other hand, combine both the probe and electronics into a single unit. These sensors are compact and convenient, making them easier to install and use in applications where space is limited.</p><p>Both types of sensors offer reliable and accurate proximity sensing capabilities, but the choice between the two depends on specific application requirements and space limitations.</p></p>
