<p><p>Cellulose gum, also known as sodium carboxymethyl cellulose (CMC), is a water-soluble polymer derived from cellulose, a substance found in the cell walls of plants. Cellulose gum serves as a thickening agent, stabilizer, and emulsifier due to its unique properties.</p><p>The growth of the cellulose gum market is driven by increasing demand from the food and beverage industry. Cellulose gum is used in the production of dairy products, baked goods, and confectionery items to improve texture, stability, and overall quality. It is also extensively used in pharmaceutical formulations for its binding and disintegrating properties.</p><p>The personal care industry is another significant consumer of cellulose gum. It is used in various products such as toothpastes, shampoos, and cosmetics due to its ability to enhance viscosity and stability.
