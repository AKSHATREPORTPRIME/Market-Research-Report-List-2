<p><p>Cardiac ablation catheters are medical devices used to treat various cardiac conditions, especially cardiac arrhythmias. These catheters are used to destroy small areas of heart tissue that may be causing abnormal electrical signals and thereby restore the heart's normal rhythm. The procedure involves inserting the catheter into a blood vessel in the groin, threading it up to the heart, and applying energy to destroy or scar the targeted tissue.</p><p>The global cardiac ablation catheters market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The market is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of cardiac arrhythmias, advancements in catheter technology, rising geriatric population, and the growing adoption of minimally invasive procedures. Additionally, the awareness about the benefits of cardiac ablation procedures and the increasing healthcare expenditure in developing regions are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in cardiac ablation catheters, such as the development of cryoablation catheters, radiofrequency ablation catheters, and microwave ablation catheters, are further propelling market growth. This growth can be attributed to the rising prevalence of cardiac arrhythmias, technological advancements, and increasing awareness and adoption of minimally invasive procedures.</p></p>

<p><p>Cardiac ablation catheters are medical devices used in the treatment of abnormal heart rhythms. There are two main types of cardiac ablation catheters: multielectrode ablation catheters and single point ablation catheters. Multielectrode ablation catheters have multiple electrodes that can deliver energy for ablation in different directions, allowing for more precise and efficient treatment. Single point ablation catheters have a single electrode and are generally used for simpler ablation procedures. Both types of catheters play a crucial role in treating cardiac arrhythmias.</p></p>
