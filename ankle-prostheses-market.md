<p><p>Ankle prostheses are medical devices used to replace a damaged or diseased ankle joint. There are two main types of ankle prostheses: cementless and cemented. Cementless ankle prostheses are designed to be fixed directly into the bone without using any cement. They rely on the bone growing into the prosthesis for stability. In contrast, cemented ankle prostheses are fixed into place using bone cement. The choice between cementless and cemented ankle prostheses depends on factors such as patient age, bone quality, and surgeon preference.</p></p>
