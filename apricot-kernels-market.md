<p><p>Apricot kernels are classified into two market types: bitter and sweet. Bitter kernels contain a compound called amygdalin, which, when ingested, can be converted into hydrogen cyanide in the body. Due to potential health risks, bitter kernels are primarily used in the production of natural remedies. On the other hand, sweet kernels have lower levels of amygdalin and are predominantly used in culinary applications. They add a nutty flavor to dishes and are commonly used in baking, cooking, and as a snack. It's important to note that bitter kernels should be consumed cautiously and in moderation, if at all.</p></p>
