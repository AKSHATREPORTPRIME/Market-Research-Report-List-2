<p><p>Neurotrophic keratitis (NK) is a rare degenerative corneal disease characterized by the loss of corneal sensitivity, impaired corneal healing, and epithelial breakdown. The condition is caused by damage to the trigeminal nerve, resulting in decreased corneal sensation and subsequent progressive corneal thinning and ulceration.</p><p>The treatment of neurotrophic keratitis focuses on promoting corneal healing, preventing complications, and improving visual outcomes. The use of artificial tears, ointments, and lubricants is commonly employed to keep the cornea moist and protect it from further damage. Additionally, bandage contact lenses and amniotic membrane grafts can be utilized to facilitate corneal healing.</p><p>Other treatment options include the use of autologous serum eye drops, which contain various growth factors that promote corneal epithelial healing. Nerve growth factor (NGF) eye drops have also shown promise in the treatment of neurotrophic keratitis. These drops help restore corneal innervation and enhance corneal healing.</p><p>The market for neurotrophic keratitis treatment is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
