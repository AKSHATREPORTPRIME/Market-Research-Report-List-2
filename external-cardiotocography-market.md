<p><p>External cardiotocography is a non-invasive procedure used for monitoring fetal heart rate and uterine contractions during pregnancy and labor. It involves the use of two sensors, one to measure fetal heart rate and the other to measure uterine contractions. These sensors are placed externally on the abdomen of the pregnant woman. The data collected from the sensors is recorded and displayed on a monitor, allowing healthcare professionals to assess the well-being of the fetus and make informed decisions during labor.</p><p>The external cardiotocography market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing number of pregnancies and the rising awareness about the importance of fetal monitoring are the key factors driving market growth.
