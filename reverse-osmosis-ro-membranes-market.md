<p><p>Reverse Osmosis (RO) membranes are used in water filtration systems to separate impurities from water through a semi-permeable membrane. There are two main types of RO membranes: cellulose-based film and thin film composite film. Cellulose-based film is made from cellulose acetate and is commonly used in residential and commercial applications due to its cost-effectiveness. Thin film composite film, on the other hand, is made from a thin polyamide layer and is known for its high efficiency and durability. Both types play a crucial role in the RO membranes market, catering to different needs and preferences.</p></p>
