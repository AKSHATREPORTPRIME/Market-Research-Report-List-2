<p><p>The nonfat milk powder market consists of three main types based on the processing treatment: low-heat, medium-heat, and high-heat. Low-heat processing treatment involves drying at lower temperatures to preserve the nutritional value and flavor of the milk. Medium-heat treatment uses slightly higher temperatures to extend the shelf life of the powder while maintaining reasonable nutritional content. High-heat processing treatment involves drying the milk at high temperatures, resulting in a longer shelf life but with diminished nutritional value compared to the other two types.</p></p>
