<p><p>Medical-grade textiles refer to fabrics specifically designed and manufactured for use in the medical and healthcare industry. There are three main types: non-woven fabric, woven fabric, and knitted fabric. Non-woven fabric is made up of fibers entangled together, providing good fluid resistance and breathability. Woven fabric is created by interlacing yarns at right angles, offering high strength and durability. Knitted fabrics are produced by intermeshing loops of yarn, providing elasticity and flexibility. All these types are essential in various medical applications, including surgical gowns, bandages, bedding, and wound dressings.</p></p>
