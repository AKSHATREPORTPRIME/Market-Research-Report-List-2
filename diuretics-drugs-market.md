<p><p>Diuretics refer to medications that promote increased urine production and reduce water retention. They are divided into two main types: oral diuretics and intravenous injection diuretics. Oral diuretics are taken by mouth and absorbed via the gastrointestinal tract before exerting their effects on the kidneys. Intravenous injection diuretics, on the other hand, are directly administered into the bloodstream, allowing for more immediate and potent effects. Both types of diuretics are commonly used to treat conditions such as hypertension, heart failure, and edema.</p></p>
