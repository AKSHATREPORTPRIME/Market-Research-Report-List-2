<p><p>Regenerative therapies encompass three main types: allografts, xenografts, and alloplasts. Allografts involve using tissues or cells from a donor of the same species, such as using donated organs for transplantation. Xenografts involve using tissues or cells from a different species, while alloplasts involve using synthetic or artificial materials to replace or repair damaged tissues. These therapies aim to stimulate the body's natural healing processes, promoting tissue regeneration and repair for various medical conditions. The regenerative therapies market is continuously growing as these techniques offer potential treatments for a range of diseases and injuries.</p></p>
