<p><p>A laser gyroscope is a device that utilizes the interference of laser beams to measure the rotation rate of an object. Its working principle is based on the Sagnac effect, where two light beams traveling in opposite directions around a closed loop experience a phase shift that is directly proportional to the rotation rate. This technology finds application in various fields such as aerospace, defense, industrial, and navigation.</p><p>The future of the laser gyroscope market looks promising, with a projected CAGR of 14.2% during the forecasted period. <p><p>The Laser Gyroscope market includes two main types: Interferometric Fiber Optic Gyroscope and Resonant Fiber Optic Gyro. Interferometric Fiber Optic Gyroscope is a type of laser gyroscope that measures changes in the interference pattern of light waves passing through a coiled fiber optic cable to detect rotation. It is known for its high accuracy and stability. On the other hand, Resonant Fiber Optic Gyro utilizes the phenomenon of light resonating in a closed loop to measure rotation. It offers a compact and lightweight alternative with good performance. Both types are used for various navigation and stabilization applications.</p></p>
