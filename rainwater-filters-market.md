<p><p>The rainwater filters market encompasses various types of filters that serve specific purposes. Mechanical pre-tank filters are designed to remove large particles and debris from rainwater before it enters the storage tank. Microscopic filters employ membranes or screens to eliminate contaminants like bacteria and sediments. Disinfection filters employ UV light or chemical treatments to kill harmful microorganisms. Carbon filters use activated carbon to remove impurities and improve taste and odor. Other types of filters include self-cleaning filters, automatic filters, and reverse osmosis filters, each with their unique filtration mechanisms.</p></p>
