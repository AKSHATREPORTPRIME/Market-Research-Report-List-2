<p><p>Open-ear headphones, also known as bone conduction headphones, are a type of audio device that use bone conduction technology to transmit sound. Unlike traditional headphones that use speakers placed over or inside the ears, open-ear headphones rest on the cheekbones or jawbone and deliver sound vibrations directly to the inner ear through the bones. This allows users to hear sound while still being aware of their surroundings and maintaining situational awareness.</p><p>The future outlook of the open-ear headphones market looks promising. With increasing awareness about the potential hearing damage caused by traditional headphones, demand for open-ear headphones is expected to rise. Additionally, the rise in popularity of augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) technologies is expected to drive the demand for open-ear headphones, as they provide an immersive audio experience without drowning out external sounds.</p><p>The current market for open-ear headphones is witnessing significant growth.
