<p><p>InGaAs cameras are categorized into two types based on the cooling mechanism: cooled cameras and uncooled cameras. Cooled cameras utilize a cooling system to lower the temperature of the detector, typically by using a thermoelectric cooler or liquid nitrogen. These cameras provide higher sensitivity and lower noise levels, making them suitable for high-end applications such as spectroscopy and scientific research. On the other hand, uncooled InGaAs cameras do not require cooling systems, making them more compact, cost-effective, and suitable for simpler applications like industrial monitoring and thermal imaging.</p></p>
